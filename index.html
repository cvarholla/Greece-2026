🔥 Firebase Sync Setup

Free. One-time. ~5 minutes. Do this on desktop if possible — easier to copy the config.

1
Create a Firebase project

Open console.firebase.google.com and sign in with your Google account. Tap Create a project (or + Add project). Enter a name like greece-trip. When asked about Google Analytics, tap Not right now (you don't need it). Tap Create project and wait ~20 seconds for it to finish, then tap Continue.

2
Enable Realtime Database

In the sidebar you just opened, tap Databases & Storage — it will expand. Tap Realtime Database. Tap the blue Create Database button. Choose any location (United States is fine) and tap Next. On the following screen select Start in test mode then tap Enable. After a moment you'll land on the database page — copy the URL shown at the top (looks like https://greece-trip-xxxxx-default-rtdb.firebaseio.com). You'll need it in step 4.

3
Register a web app & get your config

Open the sidebar again (☰ top-left) and tap Settings → Project settings. Scroll down on that page until you see a section called Your apps. Tap the </> web icon to add a web app. Give it any nickname (e.g. greece-web), leave "Also set up Firebase Hosting" unchecked, and tap Register app. You'll see a code block — scroll to find the lines with apiKey, authDomain, databaseURL etc. Copy everything from the { to the closing } and paste it in step 4. Tap Continue to console when done.

4
Paste your config here

Paste the firebaseConfig object you copied. It should include a databaseURL line — if it doesn't, go back to the Realtime Database section, copy the URL shown at the top, and add it manually as "databaseURL": "https://your-db-url".

5
Set your editor PIN

Pick a 4–6 digit PIN. Only you need to know it — family members open the same link but stay read-only unless they enter this PIN.

6

International Driver's Permit
⚠️ Required by Greek LawGreece legally requires an International Driving Permit (IDP) in addition to your US state driver's license. Get one before you leave: AAA issues them same-day for $20 with 2 passport photos. Your US license alone is technically not sufficient and could void your rental car insurance if you are in an accident.

🚗 Delphi RecommendationFor Delphi, you do NOT need the Apr 21 rental car — that's your Peloponnese car. Athens Tips

Acropolis at 8:00 AM — arrive 5 minutes early

Timed-entry is strict: your window is 15 min before/after the time on your ticket. The first slot is the best — cool, uncrowded, golden light on the marble. By 10 AM tour groups dominate the site.

National Archaeological Museum — check current opening hours for Fridays

Apr 17 is a Friday. The NAM opens at 8:30 AM with regular hours on Fridays. Schedule it for late morning (after the Acropolis descent) and be out by 3:00 PM. Tuesday the museum doesn't open until 1:00 PM — avoid that day.

South Slope descent — plan your sequence before leaving the plateau

You cannot re-enter the Acropolis main plateau after descending to the South Slope. See everything on top first (Parthenon, Erechtheion, Temple of Athena Nike, North Slope), then descend south toward the Theatre of Dionysus and Odeon.

National Garden — your daughter's highlight in Athens

Mid-April is peak bloom in Athens. The garden has koi ponds, exotic parrots, a small zoo, peacocks, and Roman mosaics in an oasis setting. Best visited in the late afternoon — combine with the Zappeion terrace for a cold drink and rest before the Panathenaic Stadium.

Delphi Tips

April 18 = International Monuments Day — everything is FREE

Every state-owned site and museum in Greece is free on April 18. This includes Ancient Delphi (site + museum, normally €12/person) and Hosios Loukas is the one exception as a privately managed monastery (€4/person still applies). Saves your party of 4 approximately €48 in Delphi entry fees alone.

Depart Athens by 6:45 AM — 15 minutes earlier than normal

Free days attract extra visitors, including locals who don't normally pay admission. Delphi being 2.5 hrs from Athens means most of the crowd surge hits the Athens sites, but get there at opening regardless. The Sacred Way and the Stadium at the top are dramatically better without crowds.

Stop at Hosios Loukas monastery en route

A 45-min detour off the E65 before Delphi, Hosios Loukas is one of the best-preserved Byzantine monasteries in Greece — 11th-century mosaics that will stop you in your tracks. Ticket is only €4. Best visited on the way to Delphi (not the return — you'll be tired).

Don't miss the Tholos of Athena Pronaia

This circular temple is 300m downhill from the main site on the opposite side of the road. Most visitors skip it entirely. It's the most-photographed ancient structure at Delphi and takes only 20 minutes to see. It is included on your combined ticket.

Arachova village — stop on the way back

The mountain village 10 minutes east of Delphi on the E65 is one of the most charming in mainland Greece. Stop for lunch at a taverna, browse the shops (local honey, wine, formaella cheese), and enjoy views over the Parnassus valley. Allow 45–60 minutes.

Peloponnese Tips

Do Mycenae in the morning, Nafplio for lunch, Epidaurus in the afternoon

This is the classic sequence for a reason. Mycenae opens at 8 AM and is best in morning light. Nafplio has the best lunch options in the Argolid and is genuinely one of the prettiest towns in mainland Greece. Epidaurus is magical in late afternoon when the light catches the marble seats.

Mycenae Lion Gate — better than you expect

The massive scale of the gate is impossible to appreciate in photos. The Cyclopean walls — built of stones so large the Greeks believed giants must have placed them — are extraordinary up close. Allow 2 hours for the site and museum combined. The Treasury of Atreus (Tomb of Agamemnon) is a separate 5-min walk down the road outside the main ticket.

Epidaurus acoustics — the whisper test

The 14,000-seat theatre from 360 BC has perfect acoustics — a coin dropped at the center of the orchestra can be heard in the highest row. Have one family member stand at the exact center of the circular stage (the orchestra) and whisper while the others climb to the top tier. It genuinely works and will be one of the most memorable moments of the trip.

Loukou Monastery — 5 minutes from your VRBO

The Monastery of Agios Ioannis Prodromos Loukou (John the Baptist) near Astros is built over the ruins of an ancient sanctuary and has a stunning cloister garden that's one of the most beautiful in the Peloponnese. It's free, quiet, and almost always uncrowded. Perfect for the morning before or after beach time near Arkadiko Chorio.

Beaches near Arkadiko Chorio

Kryoneri Beach (0.1 km away) is the closest — small, picturesque, free, pebbled. Tyros Beach (1.1 km) is slightly larger with beach bars. Paralio Astros (6 km north) has a long sandy beach and a pretty harbor with good fresh fish tavernas. For a family beach day, Paralio Astros is the best option in the area.

Ferry & Crete Tips

Arrive at Piraeus at least 45 minutes before departure

Gates E2 and E3 (for Crete ferries) are a 25-minute walk from the Piraeus metro station. There is a free port shuttle bus. Boarding typically starts 1 hour before departure. With a car, follow signs to the vehicle staging area — crew will direct you to your lane. Keep your car documents easily accessible.

Book a 4-berth cabin — worth every euro

A family cabin (~€100–180 total) means a real bed, privacy, and arriving in Chania rested instead of crumpled. The overnight crossing is 9.5 hours. Economy seats are airplane-style recliners and workable, but the cabin is dramatically better for a family with an overnight crossing and a full day of driving to Chania afterward.

Elafonissi — go early and go on a weekday

Elafonissi is one of the world's most beautiful beaches and it knows it. Mid-April is shoulder season so crowds are manageable, but arrive before 10 AM regardless. The pink sand is created by crushed seashells — it deepens in color toward the south end of the beach. The water is shallow and warm — your daughter will love the wading lagoons.

Chania Old Town — best in early morning and evening

The Venetian harbor and lighthouse are busiest midday. Walk the harbor at 8 AM before the tourist rush, then again at sunset when the lighthouse and old warehouses glow gold. The best food is in the narrow back streets behind the harbor (Splantzia quarter) — avoid the tourist-facing harbor-front restaurants and duck one block back for dramatically better food and prices.

Samaria Gorge — check opening date carefully

The gorge officially opens for the season around May 1 — exact date varies by year and conditions. In mid-April it is usually still closed for the full 16km through-hike. Check the current status at the Chania KTEL bus station or the official Samaria Gorge site before planning your day around it. If closed, the Imbros Gorge (near Sfakia) is shorter, easier, and often open earlier in the season.

Balos Beach — verify boat service in April

The boat from Kissamos port to Balos typically starts its April schedule around mid-April. Confirm the service is running before driving 60km to Kissamos. Alternatively, the rough 9km dirt road from the Gramvousa peninsula is drivable in a standard car but hard on tires and slow — the boat is far more pleasant.
